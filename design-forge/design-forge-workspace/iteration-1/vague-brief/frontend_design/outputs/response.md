# Response: "Make me a website"

## Clarification Needed

The brief "make me a website" is too open-ended to produce something meaningful. A website without purpose is decoration without function -- and even the most striking decoration needs a wall to hang on.

Before I can build something worth building, I need answers to a few questions. I have grouped them by priority:

### Must-Know (blocking)

1. **What is this website for?** A personal portfolio? A product landing page? A SaaS dashboard? A restaurant? An artist collective? A dev tool? The answer shapes every decision -- layout, tone, typography, interaction model, information architecture.

2. **Who is the audience?** Engineers? Consumers? Investors? Creatives? The audience determines reading patterns, density tolerance, device expectations, and accessibility requirements.

3. **What should a visitor DO on this site?** Sign up? Buy something? Read content? Contact you? Download an app? Every page needs a job. Without one, it is furniture in an empty room.

### Good-to-Know (shapes the aesthetic)

4. **What is the tone?** Serious and corporate? Playful and experimental? Luxurious and restrained? Raw and brutalist? This determines the entire visual language -- color, type, motion, density.

5. **Are there existing brand assets?** Logo, color palette, fonts, photography style, brand guidelines? If yes, I work within those. If no, I design from scratch.

6. **Any technical constraints?** Must it be static HTML? React? Next.js? Does it need a CMS? Is there a hosting target? Performance budget? Browser support requirements?

7. **What sites do you admire?** Even 2-3 reference URLs tell me more about your taste than a 10-page brief.

### Nice-to-Know (polish layer)

8. **How many pages/sections?** Single-page scroll? Multi-page with navigation? Dashboard with authenticated views?

9. **Content readiness?** Do you have copy, images, and assets -- or do I need to design with placeholder content?

10. **Timeline and scope?** Is this a proof-of-concept or production-ready? That changes how much I invest in responsive breakpoints, animation polish, edge cases, and accessibility depth.

---

## What Happens Next

Once you answer even questions 1-3, I can commit to a bold aesthetic direction and start building. I will not produce generic filler -- every design choice will be intentional and rooted in the answers you give.

Give me a purpose, an audience, and an action. I will give you something memorable.
