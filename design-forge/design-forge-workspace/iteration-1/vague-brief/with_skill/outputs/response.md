# Design Forge Response: "Make me a website"

## Status: Needs Direction

"Make me a website" is a blank canvas — I need a few specifics before I can produce a Design Decision Document and build something worth shipping. Without these, I'd be guessing, and guessing produces generic slop.

## 3 Questions Before I Proceed

### 1. What does this website need to DO?

Pick the closest fit:

- **Landing page** — sell a product, capture signups, drive a single action
- **Portfolio** — showcase your work (design, photography, development, etc.)
- **SaaS product site** — explain software, show features, convert to trial/signup
- **Agency / studio** — present services, case studies, team
- **Blog / editorial** — content-first, reading experience
- **Documentation** — technical reference, guides
- **Something else** — describe it

### 2. What should it FEEL like?

Pick 1-2 directions (or give me a reference URL):

- **Minimal** — clean, lots of white space, restrained palette (think: Linear, Notion)
- **Bold** — strong typography, high contrast, confident (think: Vercel, Stripe)
- **Editorial** — magazine-like, rich typography, content-driven (think: Medium, The Verge)
- **Playful** — rounded shapes, bright colors, approachable (think: Figma, Loom)
- **Corporate** — trustworthy, structured, enterprise-ready
- **Brutalist** — raw, unconventional, intentionally rough

If you have any reference sites, screenshots, or brand assets (logos, colors, fonts), share those — they're worth more than adjectives.

### 3. Who is the AUDIENCE?

- Developers? Designers? Executives? General consumers? Enterprise buyers?
- This determines tone, density, and visual language. A developer tool site is dense and technical. A consumer product site is spacious and emotional.

## What Happens Next

Once you answer these three questions, I will:

1. **Synthesize a Design Decision Document (DDD)** with exact typography (specific fonts + weights), color palette (hex codes), spacing values, layout structure, and ordered section list
2. **Build the site section by section** in Framer, screenshotting and QA-ing each section against the DDD
3. **Run a polish pass** against the anti-slop checklist and production quality standards
4. **Publish** and return the live URL

No ambiguity, no "nice blue" — every value locked before a single pixel moves.
