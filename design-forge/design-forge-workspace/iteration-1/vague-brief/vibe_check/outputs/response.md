# Vibe Check Response: "Make me a website"

## Status: HOLDING — Brief Too Vague to Commit Design Resources

The brief "make me a website" contains zero design-informing signals. Committing to a visual direction without context produces generic output — the definition of AI slop. Reduction before embellishment; clarity before aesthetics.

---

## What I Need Before I Design Anything

Two questions. That is all.

### 1. What does this website DO?

Pick one:

| Type | Example | Design Implications |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| **Personal portfolio** | Show your work, get hired | Minimal Clean or Swiss grid, project-showcase layout, 1 accent color |
| **SaaS product landing** | Convert visitors to signups | Hero + social proof + pricing, conversion-focused CTA system |
| **Business / agency site** | Establish credibility, generate leads | Corporate Modern, trust signals, service cards, contact funnel |
| **E-commerce / store** | Sell physical or digital products | Product grid, cart system, checkout flow, trust badges |
| **Blog / content site** | Publish and distribute writing | Typography-first, reading experience, content hierarchy |
| **Web application** | Tool people log into and use | Dashboard layout, component system, data-driven UI |
| **Event / launch page** | Single purpose, time-bound | Countdown, speaker grid or waitlist capture, dramatic hero |

### 2. Who is it for?

| Audience | Design Consequence |
|----------|-------------------|
| Developers | Dark-first, monospace accents, respect for density |
| Consumers (general) | Light-first, friendly, 44px+ touch targets, clear CTAs |
| Enterprise buyers | Professional, trust-forward, case studies, no playfulness |
| Creative professionals | Editorial, strong typography, portfolio-grade imagery |
| Healthcare / regulated | WCAG AAA target, maximum clarity, plain language |

---

## What I Will Deliver Once You Answer

A complete design system covering all 8+1 dimensions:

1. **Color System** — Full semantic palette with hex codes, light + dark variants
2. **Typography** — Font pairing, complete size scale, weight system
3. **Spacing** — 8px grid, component spacing tokens
4. **Components** — Buttons, cards, inputs, navigation with all states (default, hover, active, focus, disabled, loading, error)
5. **Data Visualization** — Chart types and styling (if applicable)
6. **Interaction & Animation** — Spring configs, micro-interactions, haptic patterns
7. **Responsive** — Breakpoint strategy tested at 320px through 1440px
8. **Dark Mode** — Thoughtful dark palette, not just inverted
9. **Design Token Interchange** — W3C DTCG format for implementation handoff

Plus: industry-informed design rules, anti-pattern verification, production quality checklist, component sourcing from shadcn/ui and 21st.dev where patterns exist.

## Exploration Mode Available

If you genuinely want to explore without a clear direction, say "surprise me" and I will:

- Present **3 distinct visual directions** with mockup descriptions, palettes, and trade-offs
- Use Exploration Mode constraints: 4 colors, 1 font family, 3 sizes, no animation
- Lock nothing until you confirm a direction

This is cheaper than building the wrong thing with full polish.

---

## Anti-Pattern Alert

Proceeding without answers would trigger multiple anti-patterns:

- **AI purple gradients** (Anti-Pattern #2) — Without brand identity, every AI tool defaults to purple/blue. That is commoditization, not design.
- **Generic hero with no product identity** (AI Slop Detection) — A gradient hero section with "Revolutionize your workflow" is not a website. It is a template.
- **No strategic alignment** — Vibe amplifies identity, never overrides it. No identity provided means no amplification possible.

---

## TL;DR

"Make me a website" needs two answers before design work begins:

1. **What does it do?** (portfolio / SaaS / store / blog / app / other)
2. **Who is it for?** (developers / consumers / enterprise / creatives / other)

One sentence each is sufficient. Then I will deliver production-grade design direction that matches the actual product, not a generic template.
