# Response to: "Make me a website"

I'd love to help you build a website using Forge + Framer. Before I start designing and assembling anything, I need to understand what we're building. A few quick questions:

## 1. What is this website for?
- **Personal portfolio** — showcasing your work, resume, contact info?
- **Business / landing page** — promoting a product, service, or company?
- **Blog or content site** — articles, posts, media?
- **SaaS product page** — hero, features, pricing, CTA?
- **Something else entirely?**

## 2. Who is the audience?
- Potential clients or employers?
- Customers evaluating a purchase?
- A general public audience?
- Internal team or community?

## 3. What content do you have ready?
- Do you have copy (headlines, descriptions, CTAs)?
- Do you have images, logos, or brand assets?
- Do you have a brand identity (colors, fonts, tone)?

## 4. Any references or preferences?
- Sites you admire or want to feel like?
- Dark mode, light mode, colorful, minimal?
- Any dealbreakers (things you definitely don't want)?

## 5. Scope
- Single page (landing page) or multi-page?
- Any interactive elements (forms, animations, galleries)?
- Will this need to be updated frequently?

---

**Why I'm asking instead of just building:** I could throw together a generic hero section with placeholder text right now, but you'd immediately want to change everything about it. Five minutes of alignment up front saves hours of revision. Give me even short answers to the above and I'll have a first draft on screen fast.
